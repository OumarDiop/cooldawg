What is Climate Change?

Climate change is the rising of carbon dioxide and other heat gases into the atmosphere. Which have warmed the earth and cause rising sea levels, melting snow and ice, heat events such as fires and drought, and extreme storms, rainfalls, and floods. Humans contribute to climate change. A change in global or regional climate patterns, in particular a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards a attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.We contribute to it by doing everyday things by using cars, turning lights on and off, when we heat and cool our houses. All of these things use energy and put gases into the air, which hurts our environment.
